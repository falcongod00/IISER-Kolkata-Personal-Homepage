My IISER Kolkata Academic Experience

0) Why did I go to IISER Kolkata?
In school, I was always just a student that did what I was required/expected to. While I participated in things that came my way to the best of my ability, be it academics or extra-curricular activities; I never wanted anything or trained for something specifically. Whenever I failed to achieve some personal goal or expectation of my own making however, I used to make sure I beat myself the next time.

When it was time for me to choose between commerce and science streams after 10th grade, I was interested in everything, did well in everything, and didn't want to give up anything. However, I was sure I loved sciences so I took all - Phy, Chem, Math, Bio combination.

Again, after twelfth grade, I was required to choose my path forward, and again, I did not want to give up any of the sciences. My Indian environment gave me two choices: medical and engineering and I was only sure that I wanted neither because I felt I didn't have the passion for them. Also, after tenth, my parents were falsely coaxed by society and coaching centers that I needed to be enrolled into one to succeed in life. So, they ended up wasting an exhorbitantly high amount of money for a coaching centre for nothing in return except my constant feeling of debt and isolation from the many useless classes I wasted so much valuable time at. They were incapable of coaching for any of the exams that mattered and were suggesting students to pay for a costly SAT exam (which they were coaching for instead inspite of it already being considerably easier than the Real Exams) and get admission into an NIT through a costly quota called DASA that is intended for foreigners and West-based NRIs. I was determined to prove my coaching center wrong and proving to myself that I can get into a place that I actually want solely through merit without my parents' hard earned money being wasted further.

As I was giving all the competitive exams just in case, I came across the BS-MS Program at IISERs somewhere (mostly on Quora) and liked the study all, choose later model and interdisciplinary prospects. Something in me had me register for the IISER Aptitude Test and hope that I got in.

While choosing among IISERs, I used the NIRF 2018 ranking to select my order of preference since distance and other factors were not on my mind at all. 17MS Seniors had also written good feedback of IISER Kolkata on Quora, FB, etc. On July 6, 2018, I recieved my first round offer letter from my first preference, IISER Kolkata; and I happily accepted.

1) 1st Year
My first semester was pretty chill and decent. Academically there was no pressure. We had to go to classes from 8/9 am to 5 pm and after that we explored friendships, campus, and did typical first-year things. I had fun with the whole 'unlearn and relearn' process that some profs described and practiced. We learnt same concepts as in 12th grade but with a more fundamental approach to everything. In physics, it was about the definition of 'time' itself, in math, we were completely flabbergasted when we were being introduced to the idea that we do not know anything without first accepting Peano's axioms. In chemistry, interesting detail into molecular orbital theory to give a better and more contemporary perspective into inorganic and organic chemistry plus some fun lab experiments like printing, making your own dye, and doing a fingerprint analysis; took the stage. In life sciences, we learnt about life, evolution, and some other things that were covered in 12th grade. Earth Sciences introduced a field we did not delve into in school - from Earth, to planets in general, to dinosaurs. We also learnt basic Python coding in this semester. I spent many classes asleep because LHC 107 had Air conditioning and hence more comfortable than A108, Nivedita Hall at night. Nothing makes one sleep like a boring class (but always interesting when I actually gave things a read before the exams) coupled with A/C and reclining chairs.

Second semester was a bit stricter and difficulty increased, though not beyond manageable. Instead of CS, we had Mathematical Methods which was more boring but I always scored good there somehow. This semester, I felt repelled to Chemistry mostly because I found that I wasn't patient enough for titrations in Chem Lab. I also felt reprelled to Physics because in this course, the prof. just assumed many tools and techniques that I was never formally introduced to before and hence I could never accept what was done in class. Even Phy Lab felt boring. I liked Math and ES. Life Sciences felt familiar because it was again 12th portion related and I already loved these concepts in school. Expts like DNA extraction etc were also better to me than Chem/Phy Labs. Before the endsemester exam, I actually finally gave myself time to read the physics portion and actually liked it. However, I had already opted for Earth Sci, Life Sci, and Math as my pre-majors for next year.

In my 1st sem, I also had a sociology course which was surprisingly interesting. The 2nd sem, we had an English course that was boring because I have been fairly okay with english always. It is a helpful course for students who aren't familiar with english as it will help navigate the unfortunately english-dominated science at all our Indian institutes.
2) 2nd Year
Academically, 2nd year got quite tougher than first year. Plus, IISER Kolkata's culture up till then had 2nd years organize most events, be office bearers, etc. Each of the three pre-majors had 2/3 courses, and they covered heavy portions. I also never attended classes mentally and used to do the bare minimum (showing up for attendance). We used to stay awake till late for some reason or the other, and hence, always woke up late or just in time for the 8 am class. I remember eating breakfast with Harshwardhan, entering bio lab right after the introductory lectures, and being caught by the lab assistant who always spotted us, scolded us, but never gave us away. I also remember being lax on some projects and taking others very seriously. We didn't really have many regular assignments (maybe just the three assignments for Analysis which I did at the last minute). It was not until the exams, that I actually studied to be honest. That's when I realised that I loved Analysis, and didn't care much for linear algebra. I also loved Hydrology & Geodynamics, and Dr. Sayantan Sarkar's lectures about atmosphere dynamics and climate. The other courses, I really don't remember my feelings for them. The second semester started with me turning a new page and attending all analysis classes, while trying my best to attend other classes too. We had CS again so that was nice - drawing turtle graphics and all. Sushil sir's analysis course was really nice and drew me in more. Life Sciences at this point became bland, I felt the some instructors didn't really care to teach much, while the others were teaching things that I found to be very repetitive and obvious at this point. Lab was okay. Earth Sciences was nice, especially the Earthquake-related inverse theory stuff. Halfway through this semester, COVID began and we went online. Both ES and LS Depts were gracious with marks and I honestly didn't have to study for getting perfect grades there. Only the Math profs forced us to earn our marks atleast to some extent. So, by the end of the semester, when I was asked to choose my major and was confused between ES and MA, I chose MA. Because, MA was the only subject I felt like I knew enough to know that I don't know anything. I had my worst grades in MA and yet, I felt happier with them. I knew that MA would be challenging (courtesy: seniors) and that every mark I get would be an achievement whereas I wasn't sure that was the case in others, especially LS since there it felt like marks were being thrown away. Despite warnings by profs, seniors, batchmates, and the whole 'can definitely not do anything apart from studies if you take math' advice; I chose Math.
